Sputnik I (USSR)
- Launched on October 4, 1957
- First spacecraft ever in history
- Launched by the Soviet Union
- Weighted 183.9 pounds
- It took about 98 minutes to orbit the Earth
Sputnik 2 (USSR)
- Launched on November 3, 1957
- First to carry a living animal, a dog named Laika
- Weighted around 1,121 pounds
- Contained several compartments for radio transmitters, telemetry system, a programming, and a temperature-control system for the cabin
Explorer 1 (USA)
- Launched on January 31, 1958
- First United States satellite
- Weighted around 30.8 pounds
- Primary science instrument was a cosmic ray detector designed to measure the radiation environment in Earth orbit
