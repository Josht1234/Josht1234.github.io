Milky Way Galaxy
- Its our home galaxy
- It's mass is estimated to be 400-800 billion solar masses
- The estimated number of stars in the Milky Way is atleast 400 billion
- It's diameter is 220,000 light-years
- At the center of the Milky Way Galaxy is believed to be a Super Massive Blackhole
Andromeda Galaxy
- Biggest galaxy in the local group
- It is 260,000 light-years in diameter
- It's estimated to have 1 trillion stars
- It's mass is estimated to be 400 billion solar masses
- It's the most distant object that you can see with the naked eye
- The Andromeda and Milky Way galaxy are going to collide in 5 billion years
- While Andromeda is the largest galaxy in the Local Group it may not be the most massive. The Milky Way is thought to contain more dark matter, which could make it more massive.
